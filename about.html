Hi, I'm Mariah Correa.

Currently enrolled in Business Information Technology at Red River College and expected to graduate December 2022.

I spent majority of my high school and university days studying to be a Pediatric Oncology Nurse but later found it as a profession that I soon lost interest in. Although I do still love learning about the anatomy and physiology of the human body, I didn't see myself doing it as a career for the rest of my life. I grew an interest in programming through my parents as they are both developers and would often show me the projects that they would work on. Before I joined the Business Information Technology program, I had no prior knowledge to the coding spectrum and had to teach myself all new concepts. I never saw things from a developers perspective before and how simple everyday things like a microwave came to work. Almost everything has a simple piece of code behind it to help it function which was something that became fascinating to me.

From drastically switching my career path from nursing to programming, I've realized that I like to challenge myself a lot in terms of just diving into something out of curiosity. 3 years ago I probably never would've thought to myself "Oh, I want to be a programmer." because I used to despise desk jobs and vowed to never do that for the rest of my life but now that's all I see myself doing. I've never lost sight of where I wanted to be in my life and kept pushing myself to try new things which is how I ended up here.

Unrelated to work, I enjoy throwing myself into new hobbies. Currently, I am learning korean, have taken up an interest in clay sculpting and pottery, and am learning how to dance. I tend to keep myself busy, otherwise I would feel like i'm wasting my time doing nothing. I also enjoy writing; mostly about my day to day activities and occasionally a poem if I really feel like it. I have actually been published in a book for my writing which is something that I feel most proud of myself for. Another hobby that I enjoy doing in my free time is taking care of plants. It feels very satisfying to watch them grow from nothing into something much bigger.
